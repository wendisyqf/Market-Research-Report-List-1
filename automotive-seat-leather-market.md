<p><p>The automotive seat leather market is divided into two main types: genuine leather and synthetic leather. Genuine leather is made from real animal hides, offering a premium feel, durability, and breathability, often associated with luxury vehicles. In contrast, synthetic leather, also known as faux leather or vegan leather, is manufactured from materials like polyurethane or polyvinyl chloride. It is generally more affordable, easier to maintain, and available in various designs, making it popular among budget-conscious consumers and eco-friendly options.</p></p>
